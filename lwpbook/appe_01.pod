=head1 Appendix E. Common Content Encodings

In an ideal world, the only character encoding (or, loosely, "character
set") that you'd ever see would be UTF-8 (C<utf-8>), and Latin-1
(C<iso-8859-1>) for all those legacy documents. However, the encodings
mentioned below exist and can be found on the Web. They are listed
below in order of their English names, with the lefthand side being the
value you'd get returned from C<$response-E<gt>content_charset>. The
complete list of character sets can be found at
http://www.iana.org/assignments/character-sets
(http://www.iana.org/assignments/character-sets).
